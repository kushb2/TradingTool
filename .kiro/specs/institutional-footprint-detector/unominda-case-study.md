## Analysis: Why This is NOT Accumulation

### 1. Major Support Breakdown (The Red Flag)

The most critical development: UNO Minda broke below its **200-Day Moving Average** (~₹1,145).

- Trading at ₹1,120 means the long-term trend structure is damaged
- When a stock slices through 200 DMA on high volume, it triggers:
  - Algorithmic selling
  - Long-term fund exits
  - Stop-loss cascades

### 2. Delivery Data Trap

**Observation:** Delivery percentages look tempting:
- 62% (16 Jan)
- 60% (19 Jan)  
- 51% (20 Jan)

**The Trap:** Notice the trend - as price drops faster (-0.7% → -3.0%), delivery percentage is **decreasing** (62% → 51%).

**Conclusion:** 
- Buyers are getting exhausted
- "Dip buyers" from earlier days are now trapped in losses
- This creates **overhead supply** (people waiting to sell at breakeven)
- Overhead supply acts as resistance for any bounce

### 3. What Our Algorithm Should Detect

**Expected Signal:** HOLD or WATCH-CAUTION (NOT BUY)

**Why NOT a BUY signal:**
- Price is BELOW 200 DMA (fails location gate)
- Delivery trend is DECREASING (not sustained accumulation)
- Price change > 3% (fails price stealth criterion)
- No cluster of elevated delivery days with stable price

**Key Insight:** High delivery during a sharp fall ≠ Accumulation. True accumulation happens with:
- High delivery + Flat price (stealth buying)
- Sustained delivery above baseline for multiple days
- Price near support (200 DMA), not breaking through it

## Trading Plan (For Reference)

### Scenario A: Aggressive Entry (Risky)
- **Trigger:** Stock reclaims ₹1,150 (back above 200 DMA)
- **Logic:** Would prove breakdown was a "Bear Trap"
- **Action:** Buy above ₹1,150 with stop loss at ₹1,115

### Scenario B: Conservative Entry (Recommended)
- **Trigger:** Wait for price to test ₹1,050-1,080 support zone
- **Logic:** Stock needs to find new base after 200 DMA breakdown
- **Action:** Watch first hour, look for stabilization pattern (Hammer/Doji candle)

## Lessons for Our Algorithm

1. **Delivery trend matters more than absolute value**
   - Decreasing delivery during fall = exhaustion, not accumulation
   - Our algorithm should track delivery TREND, not just spikes

2. **200 DMA breakdown is a major filter**
   - Price below 200 DMA should suppress BUY signals
   - This is already in our Phase 3 location gate

3. **Price stealth is critical**
   - Sharp price moves (>3%) with high delivery = distribution or panic buying
   - True accumulation is quiet (price flat while delivery high)

4. **Overhead supply concept**
   - After a fall with high delivery, trapped buyers create resistance
   - Algorithm should factor in recent price action context
